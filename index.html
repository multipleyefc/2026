Advancing multilingual eye-tracking-while-reading research

The MultiplEYE COST Action invites the research community to submit abstracts to its final international conference on eye-tracking-while-reading, bringing together work from psycholinguistics, computational linguistics, NLP, and eye-tracking methodology.

Date and venue

The conference will take place on 20-21 August 2026, in Skopje, Republic of North Macedonia, at the Faculty of Computer Science and Engineering, “Ss. Cyril and Methodius” University – Skopje.

Purpose and scope of the conference

The conference will present the main outcomes of a large-scale international collaboration involving more than 350 researchers, and will serve as an open forum for the wider eye-tracking-while-reading community. We warmly welcome contributions from researchers beyond the MultiplEYE network.

At the conference, we will introduce a new multilingual, parallel eye-tracking-while-reading corpus, developed through a coordinated multi-lab effort. Alongside the data, we will present a fully open, FAIR-compliant pipeline covering experimental presentation, preprocessing, metadata standards, and documentation, designed to support transparency, interoperability, and reproducibility, and to enable future reuse across research communities.

Beyond the core data resource, the conference will disseminate key results from the MultiplEYE working groups on eye-tracking-while-reading methodology in general, psycholinguistic research using eye-tracking, and leveraging eye-tracking data for NLP.

We invite submissions on all aspects of eye-tracking-while-reading, including

  • empirical studies
  • computational approaches
  • methodological contributions
  • open science perspectives.

The conference aims to foster cross-disciplinary exchange and to shape future directions in reading research and eye-tracking methodology.

Join us to engage with a diverse international community, discover new resources and methods, and contribute to the next generation of eye-tracking research!

Abstract submission system and instructions for authors

Submission options are: - Single oral presentation - Poster presentation - Symposium The submission link is here.

Abstracts for oral presentations or posters should be limited to 1 page and should contain: Title of the presentation; Author information (name, affiliation, contact info); Main body of the text (Background and aims, Methodology, Results, Discussion). Please use this abstract template form: MultiplEYE Final Conference Abstract template

Abstracts for symposia must be submitted as one single document (max. 5 pages) and should include: Title of the symposium; Information on the symposium chair(s) (name, affiliation, contact info); Synopsis of the symposium (describing the scientific relevance of the symposium and its aims); Abstracts for up to five oral presentations (prepared according to the format for regular single oral presentation abstracts), The name of a discussant (affiliation and contact info). Only one submission per symposium is permitted. Papers included in a symposium cannot be submitted separately as single oral presentations. Symposia applications will be evaluated in their entirety. Paper contributions from symposia that are not accepted may be invited as single oral presentations, only if space permits. Travel Grants

The MultiplEYE COST Action will offer a limited number of travel grants to participants accepted to the conference, with priority given to junior researchers from ITC countries. Please note that applicants must not book flights or accommodation until they have received and accepted the official e-COST invitation. The call for travel grants will be published after the participant selection process has been completed. Participants can be reimbursed for three types of expenses: long-distance travel, daily allowance, and visa costs. Long-distance travel covers journeys of 101 km or more one way and is reimbursed up to a maximum of 650 EUR for the round trip, provided that appropriate tickets or travel documents are submitted; plane tickets are eligible only if the total travel time by train would exceed seven hours. In addition, participants receive a fixed daily allowance of 150 EUR per day, independent of actual expenses, and visa fees can also be reimbursed upon submission of the relevant invoices.
